What you did, the delivery pressure, the specific actions, the impact. Shows ownership and excellence.
Story: VP/AVP stakeholders
How you communicated technical details to business leaders, managed expectations, gained trust
Story: Leading the team of 4
How you delegated, handled blockers, managed priorities, onboarded new members
Story: Production crisis
A production issue you owned end-to-end — diagnosis, fix, communication, post-mortem
💬 Leadership Questions
Tell me about a time you had to lead under pressure with tight deadlines. What did you do when things went wrong?
You received a Spot Award at Citi. Walk me through exactly what you did to earn it — specifics, not generalities.
How do you handle a situation where a team member is not performing? Have you ever had to document someone on a PIP?
You worked with VP and AVP stakeholders directly. How do you communicate technical problems and risks to non-technical senior leaders?
Describe a time you pushed back on a client's or manager's decision. How did you handle the disagreement?
Tell me about a complex deployment you managed. What could have gone wrong and what did you do to prevent it?
2. Technical Decision & Problem-Solving ▼
💬 Technical Scenario Behavioural
Tell me about a technically complex problem you solved that nobody else on the team could figure out.
Describe a situation where you had to learn a completely new technology quickly to deliver a project. (You have Kafka, Elasticsearch, Camunda stories.)
Have you ever disagreed with an architectural decision? What did you do?
Tell me about a time you identified a risk in a project before it became a problem. What was your approach?
Describe the most impactful thing you built from scratch. Why was it impactful? (Your Kafka base project, PDF→ES POC, control reports.)
Tell me about a production bug that was your fault. How did you handle it? What did you learn?
3. Career & Motivation Questions ▼
💬 Questions You Must Prepare
Walk me through your career from Infosys to HashedIn to now. What was the progression?
Why are you looking for a new role? (Answer honestly but positively — growth, product focus, scale, learning.)
Where do you see yourself in 3 years? What kind of engineer do you want to be?
What's your biggest strength as an engineer? What's an area you're actively working to improve?
Why do you want to join [company name specifically]? (Research the company before every interview.)
Do you have any questions for us? (Always have 3–4 thoughtful questions ready.)
